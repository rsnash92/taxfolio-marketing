Making Tax Digital requires HMRC-recognised software to keep digital records and submit quarterly updates. This guide helps you choose the right MTD software for your needs.

## What MTD Software Must Do

To be MTD-compatible, software must:

1. **Keep digital records** of income and expenses
2. **Calculate your tax position** accurately
3. **Submit quarterly updates** to HMRC via their API
4. **Send your final declaration** at year-end
5. **Store records securely** for the required period

HMRC maintains an official list of recognised software on gov.uk.

## Types of MTD Software

### Full Accounting Software

Complete solutions that handle everything:
- Bank connections and transaction imports
- Invoice creation and tracking
- Expense management
- Tax calculations
- HMRC submissions

**Best for:** Most self-employed individuals and landlords

### Bridging Software

Minimal solutions that:
- Connect your existing records (spreadsheets) to HMRC
- Don't provide full accounting features
- Simply transmit data in the correct format

**Best for:** Those who want to keep using spreadsheets (but adds complexity)

### Specialist Software

Industry-specific tools for:
- Construction (CIS)
- Agriculture
- Property management
- Specific trades

**Best for:** Businesses with unique requirements

## What to Look For

### Essential Features

- **HMRC recognition** - Must be on the approved list
- **Bank feeds** - Automatic transaction import
- **Quarterly submissions** - Direct to HMRC
- **Digital record keeping** - Meets legal requirements
- **Tax calculations** - Accurate and up-to-date

### Desirable Features

- **AI categorisation** - Saves time sorting expenses
- **Multiple income sources** - Self-employment + property
- **Mobile app** - Capture receipts on the go
- **Accountant access** - If you use one
- **Customer support** - Help when you need it

## Making the Right Choice

### For Simple Self-Employment

If you just need to track income and expenses and submit to HMRC:

**Recommended:** TaxFolio or HMRC's free software

You don't need complex accounting features. Focus on:
- Easy bank connections
- Good expense categorisation
- Simple interface

### For Freelancers Who Invoice

If you need to send invoices and track payments:

**Recommended:** FreeAgent or QuickBooks

Look for:
- Professional invoice templates
- Payment tracking
- Client management

### For Landlords

If you have rental properties:

**Recommended:** TaxFolio (handles SA105) or specialist property software

Ensure it covers:
- Property income reporting
- Mortgage interest tracking
- Multiple properties

### For Growing Businesses

If you're scaling or work with an accountant:

**Recommended:** Xero or Sage

Consider:
- Multi-user access
- Accountant collaboration
- Scalability

## Switching Software

Already using something? You can switch:

1. **Export your data** from current software
2. **Import historical records** to new software
3. **Connect your banks** to the new system
4. **Verify balances** match

Most software offers data import tools. Allow 1-2 weeks to get comfortable before any deadlines.
